Overview

Purpose

Technical Design is the first stage of turning an idea into something useful. It defines what the project should do, what limits it must work within, what problems it solves, and what direction the design should take.

This service area is focused on building strong project foundations before time, money, or materials are wasted.

Focus

What This Includes

Requirements

Defining what the design must accomplish, what conditions it must survive, and what standards it should meet.

Constraints

Identifying limits such as size, weight, materials, budget, tools, timeline, safety, and manufacturing method.

Direction

Creating a clear technical path so the project can move toward modelling, prototyping, testing, or documentation.

Applications

Possible Project Uses

Technical Design can support mechanical projects, CAD concepts, prototype planning, digital systems, equipment concepts, workflow systems, student engineering projects, and early Canadian innovation ideas.
